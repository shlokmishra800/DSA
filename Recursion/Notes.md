### **Recursion in Java**

**Recursion is a programming technique in which a method calls itself repeatedly to solve a problem. It helps in breaking a large and complex problem into smaller and simpler subproblems.**

---

### **Explanation of Recursion**

**A recursive method continues calling itself until a stopping condition, known as the base case, is reached. The base case prevents infinite recursion and stops the execution of further method calls.**

**Recursion mainly consists of two parts:**

1. **Base Case** → The condition that stops the recursion.  
2. **Recursive Call** → The part where the method calls itself with a smaller or modified value.

**Recursion is commonly used in problems involving mathematical calculations, searching, sorting, trees, graphs, and backtracking algorithms because it provides a simpler and more readable solution for complex tasks.**
