### 5. Assignment Operators

**Assignment operators are used to assign values to variables.  
They can also perform operations and assign the result in a single step.**

---

### Types of Assignment Operators

| Operator | Name                | Definition                                                                 | Example            |
|----------|---------------------|----------------------------------------------------------------------------|--------------------|
| =     | Simple Assignment | Assigns the value on the right-hand side to the variable on the left.      | a=     10     
            
| +=   | Add and Assign      | Adds the right-hand value to the variable and stores the result.          | a += 5 (a = a + 5) 

| -=    | Subtract and Assign | Subtracts the right-hand value from the variable and stores the result.   | a -= 5             

| *=    | Multiply and Assign | Multiplies the variable by the right-hand value and stores the result.    | a *= 5             

| /=    | Divide and Assign   | Divides the variable by the right-hand value and stores the result.       | a /= 5             

| %=    | Modulus and Assign  | Finds the remainder and stores it in the variable.                        | a %= 5             

---

### Example

```java
int a = 10;

a += 5;   // Adds 5 → a becomes 15
a -= 3;   // Subtracts 3 → a becomes 12
a *= 2;   // Multiplies by 2 → a becomes 24
a /= 4;   // Divides by 4 → a becomes 6
a %= 4;   // Remainder of 6/4 → a becomes 2

System.out.println(a); // Output: 2
```
